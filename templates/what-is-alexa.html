FAQs

What will I be asked to do?

There are a few ways you can be involved:

  • Interview: I interview you about how you interact with your Alexa, in person (ideally), or over video chat
  • Home visit: I'll spend a few hours hanging out with you at home, at a time that suits you. We'll chat a little, and I'll observe how you (and the people you live with) interact with Alexa.
  • Collaborative data analysis: together, we'll analyse the data you can get from Amazon about how you use Alexa, to see what sort of data they're collecting and what it reveals about your interactions with Alexa.
  • Survey: near the end of the project, I'll run a short survey to see how commonplace what I've discovered is among Alexa owners.

In all of these cases, I'll make sure you're happy and comfortable with what we do beforehand and during. If you want to stop at any time, or me to leave, you can do that.

Do I need to own an Alexa/Google Home device to take part?

You will either need to own an Alexa-enabled device, or a Google Home, or another device that has a voice assistant feature that stays in your home.

If the only voice assistant you regularly interact with is Siri/Cortana on your phone, I'm afraid you aren't suitable for this research.

The longer answer is that I think now is a really interesting time to study people's interactions with and views of AI â€“ because the AI most people encounter day to day is still pretty limited and, well, kind of rubbish.
